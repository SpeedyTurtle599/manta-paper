\begin{abstract}
\noindent MANTA (Modular Aero-thermo-chemical Nonequilibrium Transport Architecture) is a compressible flow solver for hypersonic aerothermodynamics in thermal and chemical nonequilibrium, written in C++23 with MPI- and Kokkos-based parallelism. Its load-bearing design idea is \emph{one data model, two topology back-ends, shared kernel mathematics}: the conserved state is stored identically for structured curvilinear multi-block and arbitrary-polyhedral unstructured meshes, and every reconstruction, gradient, and flux kernel runs over a single cell range with no branch on cell type. Every physics model, numerical scheme, and I/O backend is a pluggable component selected at runtime from an input deck. This paper documents the solver -- governing equations, thermochemical and transport models, spatial and temporal discretisations, implicit numerics and preconditioning, turbulence closures, and the staggered photon-Monte-Carlo radiation subsystem -- and presents its verification and validation evidence, organised after the AIAA G-077 guide with discretisation-uncertainty estimation per ASME V\&V 20 and a credibility self-assessment against NASA-STD-7009B. The evidence -- spatial and temporal order of accuracy by the method of manufactured solutions, geometric and discrete conservation at round-off, quantified Jacobian consistency, an empirical and linearized robustness classification of every flux function, and a formal validation suite in which each case is driven from a freestream initial condition to a converged steady state and reports its comparison error against the ASME V\&V 20 validation uncertainty from a grid-triplet Grid Convergence Index -- is regenerated on every build by an automated suite of more than sixteen hundred tests. The tiered (inviscid, laminar viscous, turbulent, and flight-scale thermochemical-nonequilibrium) benchmarks illustrate the solver's correctness, robustness, and performance portability on CPU and GPU hardware.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The aerothermodynamic design of hypersonic vehicles is governed by physics outside the reach of cold-gas, equilibrium aerodynamic analysis. Behind a strong bow shock, the gas excites molecular vibration, dissociates, and (at very high flight speeds) ionises; the relaxation times of these processes are commensurate with the flow residence time, so the gas is in thermal and chemical \emph{nonequilibrium}, and the surface heat flux, skin friction, and pressure that size a vehicle depend sensitively on how faithfully a solver represents that state~\cite{john_d_anderson_jr_hypersonic_2019}. Shock-boundary-layer interactions (SBLI) compound the difficulty: they induce localised, intense heating in separated flow that taxes both the spatial discretisation and the turbulence closure. Figure~\ref{fig:hero} shows the regime in miniature: dissociating two-temperature air over a cylinder, with the vibrational--electronic temperature lagging the translational--rotational temperature through the shock layer. This is precisely the nonequilibrium state this solver aims to capture.

Production nonequilibrium solvers such as NASA's LAURA~\cite{thompson_laura_2024} and FUN3D~\cite{anderson_fun3d_2025} have established robust implicit finite-volume methods coupled to finite-rate thermochemistry as the workhorse for this regime. MANTA is a new solver in this lineage, written from the outset for GPU execution and modern HPC architectures, with three design goals. First, \emph{performance portability}: all field arrays are device-resident, and all kernels are expressed once through Kokkos -- compiling to CUDA, OpenMP, or serial back-ends. Second, \emph{modularity}: every physics model and numerical scheme is a runtime-selectable plugin, so a case is assembled -- gas model, transport, chemistry, turbulence closure, reconstruction, flux function, time integrator, preconditioner -- entirely from a YAML file. Third, \emph{implicit-first robustness}: time advancement is delegated to the PETSc \texttt{TS} library~\cite{usdoe_office_of_science_sc_advanced_scientific_computing_research_ascr_petsctao_2025}, so the stiff source terms of two-temperature relaxation and finite-rate chemistry, and the extreme cell aspect ratios of resolved hypersonic boundary layers, impose no explicit-stability step limit. The integrated EnzymeAD library~\cite{moses_instead_2020} permits automatic differentiation of the full residual to produce a consistent Jacobian for the implicit preconditioner, and the PETSc \texttt{SNES} nonlinear solver provides line-search and trust-region globalisation.

Beyond assembling standard methods, the solver carries several artifacts of independent interest, each described where it arises and verified in Sec.~\ref{sec:vv}: a geometry-exact radial-basis TENO reconstruction~\cite{coveler_enhancing_2025} that recovers fifth order on arbitrarily stretched stencil geometry; an orbit-measure symmetry reduction whose geometric source is built from the discrete area-closure excess, so that well-balancing holds to round-off on \emph{any} mesh; an automatic-differentiation Jacobian tier -- exact flux, reconstruction, viscous-chain, and boundary-condition linearisations assembled on a wide stencil -- with a certified true-Newton pseudo-transient controller (PTC) that converges steady problems to machine zero; a joint empirical and linearized robustness classification of all fourteen flux functions, which separates carbuncle instability from nonlinear fragility and drove the redesign of the carbuncle correction; and a multiband-opacity-binning reduction of line-by-line spectra into transport-ready radiation tables.

This paper is a technical report on the solver and its correctness evidence, intended as the citable description of the full-order model in subsequent verification, validation, and reduced-order-modelling studies. Section~\ref{sec:arch} describes the architecture; Sec.~\ref{sec:models} the physical models, from perfect gas through equilibrium air to two-temperature nonequilibrium with finite-rate chemistry and the turbulence closures; Sec.~\ref{sec:numerics} the spatial discretisation, implicit numerics, and Jacobian machinery; Sec.~\ref{sec:radiation} the radiation subsystem; and Sec.~\ref{sec:infra} boundary conditions, meshing, and output. Section~\ref{sec:vv}, the largest, presents the verification and validation campaign -- organised after the AIAA G-077 guide~\cite{noauthor_guide_1998} into code verification, solution verification, and validation, with numerical uncertainty per ASME V\&V 20~\cite{noauthor_standard_nodate} and a credibility assessment against NASA-STD-7009B~\cite{noauthor_nasa_nodate}. Throughout, the description is deliberately faithful to what the solver implements; capabilities that are \emph{partial} or \emph{planned} are flagged as such.

\section{Solver Architecture}
\label{sec:arch}

MANTA is a cell-centred finite-volume solver. It is GPU-resident: all state lives in device memory, and the host participates only in I/O, initial-condition fill, and adaptation bookkeeping. Kokkos provides performance portability over shared-memory (CUDA, OpenMP, serial); GPU-aware MPI provides distributed-memory parallelism.

\paragraph{One data model, two topology back-ends.}
Every capability is implemented once against an abstract, unstructured-first data model; the structured curvilinear multi-block path is a performance optimisation, not a separate solver. \texttt{UnstructuredTopology} stores compressed-sparse-row (CSR) face adjacency and a once-built stencil table for polyhedral meshes; \texttt{StructuredTopology} exposes $ijk$ indexing, arithmetic stencils, and directional face loops that admit pencil sweeps, line-relaxation preconditioning, and transverse Gauss quadrature. A partition is a tagged union of the two, so the solver dispatches once on topology and is otherwise written against the shared abstraction, with geometric metrics (area vector, normal, centroid, volume) synthesised identically on both paths. Figure~\ref{fig:datamodel} sketches the arrangement.

\paragraph{State layout and ghost cells.}
The conserved state is a flat, device-resident structure-of-arrays $(n_\mathrm{vars}\times n_\mathrm{cells})$, addressed \texttt{data[var*nCells+cell]} for coalesced GPU access, with variable indices resolved at runtime through a layout registry (flow, thermodynamic, species, and turbulence groups). Ghost cells -- halo and boundary alike -- are ordinary cells in the range $[\,n_\mathrm{cellsOwned}, n_\mathrm{cells})$: there are no sentinel indices or boundary branches in the flux loop, every boundary condition writes only its ghost cell, and the Riemann solver at a boundary face simply receives the (owner, ghost) pair. The two topologies differ only in how ghosts are populated -- arithmetic neighbour lookup (structured) or a halo manager (unstructured). At the linear-algebra seam, the state is transposed once between this structure-of-arrays (SoA) layout and the interlaced array-of-structures (AoS) point-block layout PETSc expects, so physics kernels and preconditioners each see their natural layout.

\paragraph{Runtime factories.}
Every model and scheme is a plugin selected from the YAML case file through an abstract base class and a registration factory: reconstructions, gradient computers, Riemann solvers, physics (gas, turbulence, ...) models, boundary and initial conditions, refinement sensors, preconditioners, and the radiation transport and spectral models each have a registry. Reconstruction and gradient schemes are written once against a stencil protocol and registered on both the structured and unstructured factories, so the same smoothness-indicator mathematics serves both paths.

\paragraph{Distributed parallel execution.}
Unstructured meshes are partitioned on the dual cell-adjacency graph by PT-Scotch~\cite{chevalier_pt-scotch_2008,satanas_scotch_2026}. Halo exchange overlaps communication with computation: non-blocking transfers are posted, interior-face flux work runs in their shadow, and the exchange is finalised before seam faces. With a GPU-aware MPI, device pointers pass directly; otherwise a pinned-buffer pool host-stages the messages.

\section{Physical Models}
\label{sec:models}

\subsection{Governing equations}

MANTA solves the compressible, multi-species Navier--Stokes equations in conservative form. For $N_s$ species,
\begin{equation}
\U = \big(\rho_1,\dots,\rho_{N_s},\ \rho u,\ \rho v,\ \rho w,\ \rho E\big)^{\!\top},
\qquad
\frac{\partial \U}{\partial t} + \divg\mathbf{F}^{\mathrm{inv}}(\U)
= \divg\mathbf{F}^{\mathrm{vis}}(\U,\nabla\U) + \mathbf{S}(\U),
\end{equation}
with the standard inviscid flux, the Newtonian stress tensor
$\tau_{ij}=\mu(\partial_j u_i + \partial_i u_j - \tfrac23\delta_{ij}\divg\mathbf{u})$, and a mass-corrected Fick diffusion model that enforces $\sum_s\rho_s\mathbf{V}_s=0$~\cite{giovangigli_multicomponent_1999},
\begin{equation}
\rho_s V_{s,j} = -\rho D_s \frac{\partial Y_s}{\partial x_j}
 + Y_s \sum_{k=1}^{N_s} \rho D_k \frac{\partial Y_k}{\partial x_j},
\qquad
q_j = -\kappa\,\frac{\partial T}{\partial x_j} + \sum_s h_s\,\rho_s V_{s,j}.
\end{equation}
Because the diffusion fluxes carry no net mass, the diffusive-enthalpy term in $q_j$ is independent of the enthalpy datum and transports the formation enthalpy of dissociated atoms exactly~\cite{gnoffo_conservation_1989} -- the dominant heat-flux pathway to a cold catalytic wall in a dissociated hypersonic boundary layer.

In the Park two-temperature model~\cite{park_nonequilibrium_1989}, the vibrational--electronic energy is an additional conserved variable governed by
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial(\rho e_{ve})}{\partial t}
+ \divg\!\big[(\rho e_{ve}+p_{ve})\mathbf{u}\big]
= \divg(\kappa_{ve}\nabla T_{ve})
 - \divg\!\Big(\sum_s e_{ve,s}(T_{ve})\,\rho_s\mathbf{V}_s\Big)
 + Q_{T\text{-}V} + \sum_s \dot\omega_s\,e_{ve,s},
\end{equation}
where $Q_{T\text{-}V}$ is the Landau--Teller relaxation source. The conduction and diffusive-enthalpy terms are transport fluxes entering both $\rho e_{ve}$ and $\rho E$; the Landau--Teller exchange is internal to $T\!\leftrightarrow\!T_{ve}$ and therefore absent from $\rho E$. For $N_s=1$, the species equations collapse to single-mixture continuity.

\paragraph{A note on coordinate system reduction.}
Axisymmetric and spherically symmetric problems run on plane meridian (or radial-column) meshes through an orbit-weighted reduction: cell volumes and face areas carry the sector or steradian measure of the suppressed directions, and the reduction's geometric momentum source $(p-\sigma_{\mathrm{hoop}})\,C_c/V_c$ is built from the \emph{discrete} closure excess $C_c=(\sum_f A_f\nhat_f)\cdot\hat{\mathbf{r}}$ rather than its analytic value, so that the $O(1)$ radial imbalance the weighted face fluxes leave on a uniform state is cancelled exactly on any mesh -- a well-balancing property verified to machine precision in Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-conservation}. The formulation is reduction-generic: the spherical case reuses the axisymmetric source unchanged, and the source Jacobian carries the pressure part $(C_c/V_c)\,\partial p/\partial\U$ exactly for the implicit path. This symbol-less writing of the foundational equations permits arbitrary orbit measures or even entire coordinate systems for V\&V purposes (for instance, a toroidal reduction) without changing the solver's core.

\subsection{Gas models}

Five gas models are provided. The default \emph{calorically perfect} model uses $p=(\gamma-1)\rho e$ with configurable $\gamma$, $R=\qty{287.058}{\J\per\kg\per\K}$. The \emph{thermally perfect} model evaluates each species' $c_p$, $h$, and $s$ from the NASA nine-coefficient polynomials over three temperature ranges~\cite{mcbride_nasa_2002,mcbride_coefficients_1993}, mixes by mass fraction, and recovers temperature from internal energy by Newton iteration (tolerance $|\Delta T|<10^{-6}T$, clamp $T\in[50,5\times10^4]\,\unit{\K}$, three to ten iterations in practice). Built-in species cover the Park air sets and a GRI-derived combustion set~\cite{gregory_p_smith_gri-mech_nodate}.

Two \emph{equilibrium-air} models provide the local-thermodynamic-equilibrium limit between the perfect-gas and finite-rate tiers. The first evaluates the Srinivasan--Tannehill--Weilmuenster curve fits of NASA RP-1181 directly~\cite{srinivasan_simplified_1987}, with analytic Jacobian data for the implicit path. The second is table-driven: at setup, a Gibbs-free-energy minimiser -- element potentials over the same NASA-9 polynomials, Newton on the dual potentials with a Tikhonov-regularised symmetric solve -- computes the equilibrium composition of an arbitrary reactant mixture (default twelve-species air including ions, equivalent to NASA-9 plus argon), and tabulates the equation of state and transport coefficients on a $(\log\rho,\log e)$ grid that the flow solver interpolates. The fits, the minimiser, and the table cross-verify against one another, against NASA CEA, and against the LAURA reference implementation (Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-chem}). 

The \emph{Park two-temperature} gas models~\cite{park_nonequilibrium_1989,park_review_1993} provide high-fidelity finite-rate chemistry (Sec.\ref{sec:chem}) and vibrational--electronic energy: each diatomic carries a simple-harmonic-oscillator vibrational mode ($\theta_v=\{3395,2273,2817\}\,\unit{\K}$ for \ce{N2}, \ce{O2}, \ce{NO}), pressure follows the translational temperature, and the sound speed uses the frozen ratio of specific heats. The Landau--Teller source combines the Millikan--White relaxation time~\cite{millikan_systematics_1963} with Park's high-temperature limiting cross-section~\cite{park_review_1993} ($\sigma_v'=\qty{3e-21}{\m\squared}$).

\subsection{Transport models}

For single-species air, the default is Sutherland's law with $\kappa=\mu c_p/Pr$, $Pr=0.72$~\cite{sutherland_viscosity_1893,white_viscous_2005}. For neutral reacting air (for instance, a Park5 run), a Blottner--Eucken--Wilke model applies the Blottner viscosity curve fits~\cite{blottner_chemically_1971}, an energy-mode-split Eucken conductivity~\cite{eucken_uber_1913,svehla_estimated_1963}, and Wilke mixing~\cite{wilke_viscosity_1950}; species diffusion closes with a constant Lewis number (default $\mathrm{Le}=1.4$). Soret and Dufour effects and Stefan--Maxwell diffusion are not modelled.

For ionising 5/7/11-species air, the mixture coefficients are instead built from binary collision integrals through the Gupta--Yos closure of NASA RP-1232~\cite{gupta_review_1990}: curve-fit $\pi\bar\Omega^{(l,s)}_{ij}(T)$ integrals (Coulomb pairs scaling as $T^{-2}$, distinct resonant charge-exchange fits) feed the Yos mixture viscosity and translational conductivity, an internal-energy conductivity, and a Curtiss--Hirschfelder mixture diffusivity~\cite{giovangigli_multicomponent_1999}. With electrons present, quasi-neutrality imposes ambipolar diffusion ($D_i^a=2D_i$, the electron diffusivity ion-mass-averaged so ions and electrons carry zero net current), and charged-pair integrals carry a Debye-screened Coulomb logarithm evaluated at the local electron pressure~\cite{gnoffo_conservation_1989}. The closure assumes quasi-neutrality (no sheath physics) and a single electron temperature $T_e=T_v$. The model reproduces the RP-1232 single-species fits in the pure limit. The equilibrium-air gas models carry matching transport tiers -- NASA TM-104107 equilibrium-air viscosity and conductivity curve fits, or the tabulated model's own transport columns -- and a host-side physics-consistency gate enforces valid gas/transport/chemistry pairings at setup.

\subsection{Finite-rate chemistry}
\label{sec:chem}

Two finite-rate Park air mechanisms share one mechanism-generic kernel: \emph{Park-5} (\ce{N2}, \ce{O2}, \ce{NO}, \ce{N}, \ce{O}; five reactions) for neutral dissociating air~\cite{park_nonequilibrium_1989}, and \emph{Park-11}, which adds \ce{N+}, \ce{O+}, \ce{NO+}, \ce{N2+}, \ce{O2+}, and \ce{e-} with the full Park/Gnoffo reaction set -- associative ionisation, electron-impact ionisation and dissociation, and the charge-exchange ladder -- for ionising air above roughly \qty{7}{\km\per\s}~\cite{park_review_1993,park_assessment_1989,gnoffo_conservation_1989}. Forward rates are Arrhenius; backward rates enforce detailed balance through the Gibbs-energy equilibrium constant~\cite{mcbride_computer_1996},
\begin{equation}
k_b(T)=k_f(T)/K_c(T),\qquad
K_c=\exp\!\Big(\sum_s\nu_{r,s}\big[s_s/R_u - h_s/(R_u T)\big]\Big)\Big(\frac{p_{\mathrm{ref}}}{R_u T}\Big)^{\Delta\nu_r},
\end{equation}
where the Gibbs energies are taken from the \emph{same} NASA-9 polynomials that drive the equation of state, so chemical equilibrium coincides with the gas model's entropy maximum validated against NASA CEA~\cite{noauthor_nasacea_2026} by construction. In the two-temperature path, each forward rate is evaluated at the Park controlling temperature $T_c=T_{tr}^{\,s_r}T_v^{\,1-s_r}$ ($s_r=\half$ for heavy-body dissociation, $1$ for exchange and ionisation, $0$ for electron-impact dissociation), the backward rate retains $T_{tr}$ so detailed balance is exact at $T_v=T_{tr}$, and dissociating molecules draw a fraction $\alpha_d=0.3$ of their bond energy from the vibrational mode. The stiff species-block source Jacobian is assembled by finite difference in mass-fraction space; the kernel additionally exposes split forward/backward molar rates and a frozen-rate Jacobian so that partially-stirred-reactor and eddy-dissipation-concept turbulence--chemistry closures can scale the mean source without re-deriving the kinetics.

At a catalytic wall, the same kinetics close the surface boundary condition: a partial $\gamma$-model balances the wall-normal diffusive atom flux against kinetic recombination through a Damk\"ohler number,

\begin{equation}
(\rho Y_A)_g=(\rho Y_A)_i\,\frac{1-\mathrm{Da}_A}{1+\mathrm{Da}_A},
\qquad \mathrm{Da}_A=\frac{\gamma_A\sqrt{R_A T_w/(2\pi)}\,\delta}{D_A},
\end{equation}

recovering the fully- and non-catalytic limits as $\gamma_A\to1,0$ with one homonuclear channel per atom~\cite{wright_data_2009}.

\subsection{Turbulence closures}
\label{sec:turb}

A single closure interface serves RANS, LES, and hybrid models. The RANS set comprises Spalart--Allmaras (with all sensor functions differentiated for the implicit diagonal source Jacobian, verified against central differences)~\cite{spalart_one-equation_1992}; Menter SST $k$--$\omega$ with the $F_1$ blending and shear-stress-limited eddy viscosity~\cite{menter_two-equation_1994,wilcox_turbulence_2006}, plus the vorticity-production SST-V variant that suppresses stagnation-point energy build-up~\cite{menter_ten_2003}; the 2024--2025 GEKO revision, whose independent coefficients tune separation, near-wall, free-shear, and jet behaviour without disturbing the wall calibration~\cite{menter_generalized_2025}; and the Launder--Reece--Rodi Reynolds-stress closure for anisotropy under strong curvature and three-dimensional separation~\cite{launder_progress_1975}. The LES set comprises WALE (correct near-wall cubic scaling without a wall distance)~\cite{nicoud_subgrid-scale_1999}, classical Smagorinsky--Lilly~\cite{smagorinsky_general_1963,lilly_application_1966}, and a dynamic one-equation subgrid model with a Germano-identity coefficient~\cite{kim_new_1995,germano_dynamic_1991}. The hybrid SA-IDDES model reuses the SA transport equation with a hybrid length scale -- RANS in attached boundary layers, wall-modelled LES on resolved near-wall grids, LES in separated regions~\cite{shur_hybrid_2008,spalart_new_2006}. Turbulent heat and species fluxes close with turbulent Prandtl and Schmidt numbers ($Pr_t=0.9$, $Sc_t=0.7$ by default), with an optional Kays--Crawford correlation for the near-wall rise in $Pr_t$~\cite{kays_turbulent_1994,kays_convective_2005}. This menu of closures is validated against canonical benchmarks in Sec.~\ref{sec:vv}, and provides a testbed to independently reproduce SBLI capture and separation studies~\cite{maclean_ground_2008, wadhams_ground_2008}.

\section{Numerical Methods}
\label{sec:numerics}

\subsection{Finite-volume formulation}

Integrating the conservation law over a cell and applying the divergence theorem gives the semi-discrete system:

\begin{equation}
V_i\,\frac{\mathrm{d}\U_i}{\mathrm{d}t}
= -\sum_{f\in\partial V_i}\hat{\mathbf{F}}_f\,A_f + V_i\,\mathbf{S}_i.
\label{eq:semidiscrete}
\end{equation}

The structured path loops over the $(\xi,\eta,\zeta)$ directions and the unstructured path over a CSR face list; the per-face computation is identical. Left and right face states are obtained by reconstruction and passed to the selected Riemann solver. Metrics are synthesised identically on both paths -- a Newell area vector and a pyramid-decomposition volume for arbitrary polyhedra. On structured faces, the flux integral may be raised to a tensor-product Gauss--Legendre rule of up to three nodes per tangent axis (the default single node is byte-identical to a midpoint sweep); the unstructured path integrates at the face centroid only. \emph{This face quadrature -- not the reconstruction -- is what caps the assembled spatial operator at second order for general multidimensional flow}, a property Sec.~\ref{sec:vv} measures rather than assumes.

Cell-centre gradients feed the viscous fluxes and higher-order reconstruction. The menu mirrors production practice: plain and distance-weighted Green--Gauss, an inverse-distance-weighted least-squares operator with the mesh-only normal matrix precomputed (linearity-exact on any mesh, recommended for wall gradients~\cite{barth_design_1989,venkatakrishnan_application_1989,mavriplis_revisiting_2003}), node-based Green--Gauss and least-squares variants with linearity-preserving Holmes--Connell weights~\cite{holmes_solution_1989} (seam-node neighbourhoods sum-reduced across MPI ranks and blocks), and a structured-only fourth-order central operator on uniform blocks.

\subsection{Riemann and flux functions}

MANTA implements the fourteen flux functions of Table~\ref{tab:riemann} behind a single explicit contract; the assembled implicit Jacobian is built separately -- by default an approximate Steger--Warming split independent of the flux choice, or an exact derivative of whichever flux is selected (Sec.~\ref{sec:jacobian}) by Enzyme auto-differentiation. The two-wave HLL family~\cite{harten_high_1983} differs only in wave-speed estimate (Davis bounds~\cite{davis_simplified_1988}; Einfeldt's positivity-preserving Roe-averaged speeds~\cite{einfeldt_godunov-type_1988,einfeldt_godunov-type_1991}); HLLEM restores the linearly degenerate fields by a Dumbser--Balsara projection~\cite{dumbser_new_2016} and HLLC restores the contact wave~\cite{toro_restoration_1994}. Low-Mach variants rescale the normal-velocity jump by a Mach sensor to remove spurious acoustic dissipation as $M\to0$~\cite{rieper_low-mach_2011,thornber_improved_2008,fleischmann_shock-stable_2020}, and AUSM$^+$-up splits convective and pressure parts for all-speed accuracy~\cite{liou_sequel_1996,liou_sequel_2006}. The Roe solver carries a Harten entropy fix~\cite{roe_approximate_1997}, Rusanov is the maximally robust baseline~\cite{rusanov_calculation_1962}, and an entropy-stable flux pairs Chandrashekar's kinetic-energy-preserving two-point flux~\cite{chandrashekar_kinetic_2013} with Ismail--Roe/Winters matrix dissipation~\cite{ismail_affordable_2009,winters_affordable_2016} for a discrete entropy inequality at far lower contact dissipation than scalar Lax--Friedrichs. An iterative exact Godunov solver~\cite{toro_riemann_2009} completes the set as the zero-model-error reference against which the robustness classification of Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-robust} is measured.

Three solvers target the carbuncle instability and positivity at strong hypersonic shocks. Rotated-RHLL~\cite{nishikawa_very_2008,ren_robust_2003} decomposes the face normal into a shock-normal direction (dissipative HLLE) and a transverse direction (low-dissipation Roe), collapsing to a Roe flux with modified wave speeds. The Bouchut three-wave solver replaces the pressure law with the linearly degenerate Suliciu relaxation system~\cite{suliciu_modelling_1990,chalons_relaxation_2008}, whose Riemann problem is solved exactly and which is \emph{provably positive} for arbitrarily strong shocks under the subcharacteristic condition~\cite{bouchut_nonlinear_2004}; the rotated-Bouchut variant combines carbuncle resistance with that positivity guarantee at the cost of two flux evaluations per face. All shock-capturing reconstructions operate on local characteristic variables built from the mean acoustic impedance of the face-adjacent states; passive scalars are reconstructed directly.

\subsection{High-order reconstruction}
\label{sec:recon}

Reconstruction maps cell averages to face states. The minmod-limited MUSCL scheme~\cite{van_leer_towards_1979} is the second-order shock-capturing baseline. Fifth-order accuracy is provided by Jiang--Shu WENO5~\cite{jiang_efficient_1996,shu_efficient_1988} and its critical-point-corrected WENO5-Z variant~\cite{borges_improved_2008}, and by the TENO family~\cite{fu_family_2016,fu_review_2023}, which replaces smooth weighting with a sharp binary cutoff on a scale-independent sensor: candidate sub-stencils are fully retained or discarded before optimal-weight recombination, giving complete stencil de-selection across discontinuities while preserving the low-dissipation full stencil in smooth turbulence. TENO5-A adapts the cutoff threshold locally~\cite{fu_very-high-order_2019}. TENO5-RBF derives per-face sub-stencil weights from a polyharmonic-spline radial-basis interpolation of the cell averages on the face-normal projection of the \emph{actual} stencil geometry~\cite{coveler_enhancing_2025}: with full quadratic monomial augmentation, the saddle system collapses to the unique geometry-exact polynomial reconstruction, recovering fifth order on arbitrarily stretched projected geometry and reducing exactly to classical TENO5 on a uniform grid. A non-dissipative fourth-order central scheme~\cite{leveque_finite_2002} is provided for DNS/LES of smooth turbulence, and a startup decorator blends from first order during cold-start without relaxing the converged discretisation~\cite{richtmyer_proposed_1948}.

\subsection{Implicit time integration}
\label{sec:temporal}

MANTA is implicit-first. The production integrator is PETSc \texttt{TS} BDF2~\cite{hairer_solving_1996}, with backward Euler as startup fallback, a stiffly accurate third-order ESDIRK (\texttt{es324sal}) as alternative, and explicit SSP-RK3~\cite{gottlieb_strong_2001} retained for verification. At each step, PETSc solves the shifted system $(\alpha M - \partial\mathbf{R}/\partial\U)\,\delta\U = -\mathbf{G}(\U)$ with the shift $\alpha$ supplied by \texttt{TS}, model-independent across BDF2, backward Euler, or each DIRK stage. The Krylov operator is the PETSc matrix-free finite-difference Jacobian (Jacobian-free Newton-Krylov, or JFNK), which differences the full nonlinear residual -- so the exact linearisation of whichever flux is active is captured without per-solver Jacobian code -- with the Walker--Pernice step~\cite{pernice_nitsol_1998,dennis_numerical_1996}
\begin{equation}
h=\frac{\varepsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}\sqrt{1+\lVert\U\rVert_2}}{\lVert\mathbf{x}\rVert_2}
\end{equation}
scaled by both the state and the Krylov direction, holding the perturbation above round-off across the many-decade dynamic range of dimensional hypersonic states. When the assembled operator of Sec.~\ref{sec:jacobian} is exact for the active configuration, it can instead serve as the Krylov operator directly, replacing the matrix-free differencing and permitting even faster convergence at the cost of more memory.

\subsection{Jacobian assembly: approximate splits and exact automatic differentiation}
\label{sec:jacobian}

The assembled matrix is, by default, a preconditioner-grade approximation on the compact nearest-neighbour stencil. Its convective part is a unified Steger--Warming split~\cite{steger_flux_1981} over the whole conserved vector, following the DPLR family~\cite{maccormack_solution_1989,wright_data-parallel_1998}, evaluated from Frobenius covariants with a Harten entropy fix, with a Roe-matrix linearisation as the alternative; the block-diagonal source Jacobian (two-temperature relaxation, chemistry, turbulence) is added on the cell diagonal, roughly halving the Krylov count on vibration-stiff solves. This tier is deliberately approximate in three ways -- the split is not the derivative of the selected flux, the first-order stencil is not the derivative of the high-order residual, and boundary rows close with a ghost-equals-owner response -- and each of those distances is measured, not assumed, in Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-jac}.

When exactness is wanted, an automatic-differentiation tier assembles the true Jacobian of the discrete residual. In builds carrying the Enzyme LLVM plugin~\cite{moses_instead_2020, moses_reverse-mode_2021, moses_scalable_2022}, forward-mode passes differentiate the \emph{actual} pointwise kernels -- no hand linearisation, and nothing to re-derive when a model is swapped: the selected Riemann flux ($\partial\hat{\mathbf{F}}/\partial\U_{L,R}$ of whichever entry of Table~\ref{tab:riemann} is active), the MUSCL reconstruction (differentiated live through the limiter seam, or replayed frozen from recorded face states), the full viscous chain -- the face gradients' dependence on every stencil cell carried exactly through a reified sparse gradient operator -- and each nonlinear boundary condition's ghost response (no-slip wall, farfield, subsonic inflow and outflow; linear conditions are filled analytically). A wide-stencil sparsity pattern chains the per-face flux blocks through the reconstruction and gradient adjacency into an interlaced block-COO fill -- a 13-point star per row for MUSCL, growing further for 5-point stencils -- and boundary ghost columns are folded algebraically onto their interior donors, so boundary rows are exact rather than approximated. Every row of the resulting operator matches Fr\'echet derivatives of the residual, inviscid and viscous alike (Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-jac}); the same AD blocks cross-check the finite-difference Jacobians of the robustness analysis of Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-robust} and price each flux function's conditioning toward vacuum. Figure~\ref{fig:spy} contrasts the two sparsity patterns on a small mesh.

The shifted operator is preconditioned by topology. The structured default is alternating-direction (Beam--Warming) line relaxation~\cite{beam_implicit_1976,pulliam_diagonal_1981} -- three block-tridiagonal sweeps solved exactly by block Thomas -- with an opt-in DPLR plugin~\cite{wright_data-parallel_1998} that treats the stiff wall-normal direction exactly and relaxes cross-stream couplings by multi-coloured Gauss--Seidel~\cite{yoon_lower-upper_1988}. The unstructured default is PETSc ILU(0), with a setup-free multicolour LU-SGS smoother~\cite{yoon_lower-upper_1988,sharov_reordering_1998} whose greedy colouring makes each colour update race-free; algebraic multigrid (GAMG as unsmoothed aggregation~\cite{briggs_algebraic_2000,cleary_robustness_2000}) and the GPU-portable PETSc back-end~\cite{mills_toward_2021} are also available.

\subsection{Pseudo-transient continuation and the true Newton solve}
\label{sec:ptc}

Steady-state runs use pseudo-transient continuation~\cite{mulder_experiments_1985,crivellini_implicit_2011}: a linear CFL ramp followed by a switched-evolution-relaxation update that grows the pseudo-step against the residual history: 

\begin{equation}
\Delta\tau^{n+1}=\Delta\tau^{n}\,\mathrm{clamp}[(\lVert\mathbf{R}^{n-1}\rVert/\lVert\mathbf{R}^{n}\rVert)^{\alpha},0.1,10]
\end{equation}

with per-cell local pseudo-timestepping available for boundary-layer-stiff cases. Convergence is declared per governing-equation group (continuity, momentum, energy, vibrational, species, turbulence), each normalised by its running maximum so the large-magnitude energy residual cannot mask an unconverged continuity or turbulence equation. A full-approximation-scheme nonlinear multigrid initialiser on agglomerated coarse levels~\cite{brandt_multi-level_1977,mavriplis_convergence_1998} provides a startable cold-start field, with a $\tau$-correction that leaves an already-converged fine field exactly fixed.

The exact operator changes what pseudo-time can do. At setup, the assembler \emph{certifies} whether the assembled Jacobian is exact for the active configuration -- wide stencil, AD convective and viscous blocks, every boundary row claimed by an exact ghost response, no frozen couplings -- and when it is, the controller enters a true-Newton mode: once the residual crosses a threshold, it stops throttling by the SER/CFL heuristic and grows the pseudo-step geometrically toward the Newton limit, backing off and cooling down on a failed solve so that the approach is a continuation in the shift rather than a cliff. Certified steady problems converge to machine zero in a handful of terminal iterations; an uncertified configuration simply retains the classical SER dynamics. Figure~\ref{fig:endgame} shows the signature on an exemplar viscous case: the SER-throttled history plateaus at its heuristic floor, while the true-Newton solve's geometric pseudo-step growth turns the same solve into a terminal drop.

\subsection{Robustness and limiting}
\label{sec:robust}

Positivity is guarded by density and pressure floors (defaults $\rho_{\min}=\qty{e-8}{\kg\per\cubic\m}$, $p_{\min}=\qty{e-3}{\Pa}$) applied in a minimum-correction, velocity-preserving fix at both the reconstructed face and the updated cell; transported turbulence scalars carry analogous realizability bounds ($k\ge0$, $\omega\ge\omega_{\mathrm{floor}}>0$, an eddy-viscosity-ratio cap). On grid-aligned strong shocks, the contact-resolving solvers may be protected by an opt-in multidimensional H-correction~\cite{sanders_multidimensional_1998}: a cell-based compressive-shock sensor (silent on shear and rarefactions) injects only the \emph{excess} transverse Rusanov dissipation on shock-parallel faces -- where the odd--even carbuncle mode actually grows -- and leaves shock-normal faces untouched. The correction was redesigned from a face-local pressure blend after the V\&V suite's linearized stability analysis showed the original sensor silent on exactly the faces that carry the instability; both the diagnosis and the fix are gated in Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-robust}. The rotated and Bouchut solvers address the carbuncle structurally. For steady boundary-layer runs a cell-aspect-ratio scaling lifts each cell's local pseudo-step by its directional aspect ratio (capped), and the step diagnostics report both the convective Courant number and the viscous von~Neumann number so a diffusion-limited regime is visible rather than silent.

\section{Radiation Transport}
\label{sec:radiation}

At atmospheric entry velocities, the shock layer radiates strongly, and radiative cooling can rival convective heating at the stagnation point~\cite{johnston_nonequilibrium_2008}. MANTA's radiation subsystem is factored into two independently selectable axes: a \emph{spectral} model supplying per-band emission $j_b$ and absorption $\kappa_b$, and a \emph{transport} model determining how much of that emission escapes, is reabsorbed, or reaches a wall. Along a path the intensity obeys the radiative transfer equation for a non-scattering medium, whose exact formal solution across a homogeneous segment:

\begin{equation}
I_\nu^{\mathrm{out}}=I_\nu^{\mathrm{in}}e^{-\kappa_\nu\Delta s}+({j_\nu}/{\kappa_\nu})(1-e^{-\kappa_\nu\Delta s})
\end{equation}

is the building block of both the transport tiers and their verification. The radiative source enters the total (and, in a two-temperature solve, vibrational--electronic) energy equation as $-\divg\mathbf{q}_{\mathrm{rad}}$; in the optically thin limit, reabsorption is dropped and the source collapses to the local band-summed emission -- the one term for which $\rho E$ is deliberately \emph{not} conserved.

\paragraph{Spectral models.}
A grey tier and a synthetic multiband tier exercise the band machinery; the production tier interpolates an opacity table reduced offline from NEQAIR line-by-line air spectra~\cite{johnston_spectrum_2008,lopez_spark_2016}, storing mass-specific emission and absorption coefficients per species and band on a $(T_{tr},T_{ve})$ grid, recombined at runtime from the partial densities. The offline reduction is multiband opacity binning (MBOB), not a single grey opacity per band: a grey band smears strong-line absorption and renders the line-rich vacuum-ultraviolet optically thick, collapsing its emergent radiance. MBOB splits each band into bins by the \emph{magnitude} of $\kappa_\eta$ on globally fixed log-spaced thresholds, storing the source-weighted mean opacity
\begin{equation}
\bar\kappa_{b,g}=\frac{\int_{(b,g)} j_\eta\,\mathrm{d}\eta}{\int_{(b,g)}(j_\eta/\kappa_\eta)\,\mathrm{d}\eta}
\end{equation}
that preserves the in-band opacity distribution~\cite{hirata_improvement_2014}; each bin is downstream just another band, so the transport solver is unchanged. Figure~\ref{fig:mbob} illustrates the construction on a line spectrum.

\paragraph{Photon Monte Carlo transport.}
The participating-media tier resolves reabsorption by a deterministic, energy-conserving photon Monte Carlo: per band, each cell emits equal-energy bundles in isotropic directions from a counter-based RNG keyed by the global cell identifier (paths are reproducible independent of the partition), marched cell-to-cell with analytic per-segment deposition and attenuation. The estimator is unbiased with error $\propto N_p^{-1/2}$. Because the dense stochastic radiative Jacobian is incompatible with the sparse flow Jacobian, the source is \emph{lagged}: a producer traces rays on the converged state at a set cadence and caches a frozen source that a cheap applier adds to every residual, converging to the same steady fixed point as tight coupling. Ray tracing is parallelised by optical-domain replication decoupled from the flow decomposition -- each rank traces its own photons against the gathered global geometry and partial fields are all-reduced, a rank-count-invariant result. When the tracer is active, boundaries join the photon field as grey, diffuse, opaque surfaces: a face of emissivity $\varepsilon_f$ emits $\varepsilon_f\sigma T_w^4 A_f$ (band-partitioned by the blackbody fraction), absorbs the fraction $\varepsilon_f$ of incident power, and reflects the complement diffusely, closing the radiative-equilibrium wall balance. Independently of the stochastic tracer, the spectral tier is exercised by a deterministic tangent-slab reduction -- the same one-dimensional geometry NEQAIR runs -- so a discrepancy against a line-by-line reference isolates the spectral table's error from the flowfield's (Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-rad}).

\section{Boundary Conditions, Meshing, and Output}
\label{sec:infra}

\paragraph{Boundary and initial conditions.}
All boundary conditions are ghost-cell implementations. The no-slip wall reflects the interior velocity with adiabatic, isothermal, or radiative-equilibrium thermal modes -- the last solving $\varepsilon\sigma T_w^4=\kappa(T_i-T_w)/\Delta n$ by Newton--Raphson -- and carries the catalytic $\gamma$-model of Sec.~\ref{sec:chem}. Symmetry/slip walls mirror momentum; supersonic inflow/outflow prescribe or extrapolate; subsonic and unified farfield conditions branch on the local normal Mach number using Riemann invariants with total-condition or back-pressure closures~\cite{poinsot_boundary_1992}. A transpiration condition supports receptivity studies~\cite{fedorov_transition_2011}, translational periodicity is handled at the connectivity level, and the two-temperature path stamps equilibrium vibrational energy at inflows. Initial conditions are a uniform freestream, a per-cell expression of the centroid coordinates, or a piecewise predicate-guarded fill, with the optional full-multigrid cold start as in Sec.~\ref{sec:ptc}.

\paragraph{Mesh formats and partitioning.}
MANTA reads CGNS-HDF5 (structured blocks or face-based polyhedral zones)~\cite{poirier_cgns_1998,hauser_parallel_2004}, Fluent CFF polyhedral HDF5~\cite{noauthor_fluent_nodate}, and Gmsh MSH~4.1~\cite{geuzaine_gmsh_2009} external formats. It also contains a comprehensive suite of in-house meshing algorithms, including uniform-Cartesian, semi-auto-blocked curvilinear-block-structured, and arbitrary-polyhedral constrained-Voronoi-tesselation/restricted-Delaunay fill algorithms. All of these are wrapped behind a single interface and share a common tagging/condition-binding model, so a single input deck can drive any mesh format behind the abstraction layer.

Unstructured meshes are partitioned by PT-Scotch on the dual graph; structured multi-block meshes by whole-block round-robin or longest-axis greedy bisection, with intra-zone interface pairs emitted so intra- and inter-zone halo exchange are identical. The adaptive path uses a family-atomic space-filling-curve partition over a replicated refinement forest, so every collective is a deterministic function of the forest keys and checkpoint/restart works on any rank count~\cite{warren_parallel_1993,burstedde_p4est_2011}.

\paragraph{Adaptive mesh refinement.}
MANTA performs conservative cell-based $h$-refinement on a 2:1-balanced forest over the base mesh~\cite{berger_local_1989,burstedde_p4est_2011}. A marking pass combines sensors by pointwise maximum, applies hysteresis, and dilates the refined band as a moving-feature margin, optionally under a global cell budget; the dimensionless L\"ohner second-derivative sensor~\cite{lohner_adaptive_1987} and an expression sensor (doubling as region forcing) are built in. State transfer is a single SFC merge walk -- copy on equal keys, prolong by injection, restrict by the volume-weighted mean, exactly conservative. Hexahedra subdivide on the trilinear corner lattice; general polyhedra refine through a vertex macro split into dyadic hexes~\cite{catmull_recursively_1978}. A distributed adapt event performs seam-symmetric balancing, a bitwise-identical plan allgather, rank-local transfer, and an SFC re-cut, giving serial/parallel residual equivalence to round-off. The structured path instead adapts by block tiles consumed directly by the ADI/DPLR preconditioners, with single-rate refluxing restoring conservation at coarse-fine seams~\cite{grete_parthenonperformance_2023}.

\paragraph{Output and surface quantities.}
Snapshots are written in VTKHDF with full polyhedral connectivity and collective MPI-IO; a separate HDF5 checkpoint stores the conservative state and refinement forest, validates a mesh checksum on restart, and supports any rank count. Wall patches are extracted as a surface series reporting
\begin{equation}
C_p=\frac{p-p_\infty}{\half\rho_\infty V_\infty^2},\quad
C_f=\frac{\lVert\boldsymbol\tau_w\rVert}{\half\rho_\infty V_\infty^2},\quad
\mathrm{St}=\frac{q_w}{\rho_\infty V_\infty c_{p,\infty}(T_{0,\infty}-T_w)},\quad
y^+=\frac{\rho_w u_\tau\,\Delta n}{\mu_w}.
\end{equation}

\section{Verification and Validation}
\label{sec:vv}

\subsection{Framework and provenance}

Verification and validation are continuous activities embedded in the automated test suite: every order-of-accuracy study, conservation check, and benchmark comparison below is an assertion that runs on every change, so the evidence is regenerated on each build rather than assembled once. The campaign is organised after the AIAA G-077 guide~\cite{noauthor_guide_1998,oberkampf_verification_2010} into pillars kept deliberately separate: \emph{code verification} (order of accuracy, primarily by the method of manufactured solutions), \emph{solution verification} (grid-convergence uncertainty by the Richardson-based Grid Convergence Index), and \emph{validation} (comparison against analytic, benchmark, and experimental references along the G-077 building-block hierarchy). The solution-verification GCI furnishes the numerical uncertainty $U_{\mathrm{num}}$ that feeds the validation uncertainty $U_{\mathrm{val}}=\sqrt{U_{\mathrm{num}}^2+U_{\mathrm{input}}^2+U_D^2}$ of ASME V\&V~20~\cite{noauthor_standard_nodate,celik_procedure_2008}; Sec.~\ref{sec:credibility} closes with a credibility self-assessment against NASA-STD-7009B~\cite{noauthor_nasa_nodate}.

Every number quoted below is recorded by the passing suite on the build of record (revision \texttt{a097bb2}, AppleClang/Release, IEEE double precision), with the multi-rank subset re-run under \texttt{mpirun -n} and adapting to the optimal partition count for each volume decomposition.

The validation suite follows one convention throughout, stated here once. Every case is driven from a freestream initial condition to a residual-converged steady state (a six-order spatial-residual drop), never initialised from the reference solution and never a fixed-time snapshot. The only intelligent initialisation permitted is the aforementioned full-grid initialisation, which runs \emph{separately} for each of the GCI grids. Each reports the ASME V\&V 20 comparison error $E_v=S-D$ against the validation uncertainty $U_{\mathrm{val}}$, with $U_{\mathrm{num}}$ taken from a systematically refined three-grid triplet through the Roache GCI, $U_{\mathrm{input}}=0$ (prescribed freestreams), and $U_D=0$ for exact references or the documented scatter for empirical ones. Gates assert the real physical target at a physically justified tolerance and stay \emph{red} until met -- a validation test that goes green at partial agreement would silently assert a capability the solver does not have, so tolerances are never loosened to pass. For example, the NEQAIR flight-radiance comparison of Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-rad}, is work in progress and therefore excluded from the below claims.

\subsection{Code verification: spatial order of accuracy}
\label{sec:vv-spatial}

MANTA is by design a high-order reconstruction front-end on a second-order finite-volume back-end, and the campaign measures the two separately. The reconstruction operators are verified in isolation against the exact face value of a smooth sinusoid ($L_\infty$ norm, grids $32$--$64$--$128$); Table~\ref{tab:recon_order} shows every scheme at its design order, with the fifth-order family reaching its asymptote to within one percent of the design rate. The \emph{assembled} spatial operator is then verified per topology by the method of manufactured solutions on the smooth field
\begin{align}
\rho &= \rho_0 + A_\rho\sin(2\pi x)\sin(2\pi y), &
u &= u_0 + A_u\sin(2\pi x)\cos(2\pi y), \notag\\
v &= v_0 + A_v\cos(2\pi x)\sin(2\pi y), &

\label{eq:mms}
\end{align}
whose analytic source (plus a constant-$\mu$ viscous source for the Navier--Stokes variant) is cell-averaged with a nine-point Gauss--Legendre rule and imposed through exact-MMS ghost states. Three observations from Table~\ref{tab:spatial_order} merit note. First, the second-order cap is a property of the face quadrature -- a single midpoint flux per face on the unstructured path, a bilinear transverse blend on the structured path -- and not of the reconstruction: raising the structured tensor-product rule to three Gauss points leaves the rate at two, while a sweep-aligned (transverse-invariant) flow, for which the blend is exact, realises the full fifth order of the front-end in the assembled operator. Each high-order full-operator test carries a deliberate upper-bound gate ($\hat p<3$) that will fire when a planned higher-order back-end quadrature is implemented, forcing the gate to be re-pointed rather than silently passed. Second, high-order reconstruction still pays at fixed mesh even where the asymptotic order is capped: on the advecting isentropic vortex (which isolates spatial error by a single residual evaluation on the cell-averaged exact state), the fifth-order front-end lowers the error constant by more than an order of magnitude relative to MUSCL at $64^2$. Third, the rates survive mesh distortion -- a smooth curvilinear node warp of amplitude $0.04$ holds $\hat p\approx2$ on both topologies -- and constructing that case surfaced and fixed a latent factor-of-two cell-volume double-count in the unstructured metric synthesis, a concrete return on the MMS investment. A random per-node skew is deliberately avoided: a residual-based MMS supraconverges (reads order zero) on meshes equally skewed at every level, so the warp is smooth by construction.

On fully-polyhedral geometry, the mesh-generation battery carries its own MMS gates: the Green--Gauss gradient holds $\hat p = 1.98$--$1.99$ at the co-spherical junction cells of a graded Voronoi lattice, while on the boundary-layer-to-polyhedral transition row of a sphere-cone mesh the least-squares gradient sustains first-order convergence ($\hat p\approx0.96$--$0.99$) where plain Green--Gauss stalls at order zero -- an assertion the suite keeps \emph{inverted} as a living tripwire, documenting a known scheme limitation.

Order preservation across a 2:1 adaptive-refinement interface is measured rather than assumed, on a mesh family whose refined patch is geometrically self-similar under base refinement and with a uniform-mesh control. In the \emph{residual} study the bulk holds the design order ($\hat p=1.82$, gated $\ge1.8$) while the fixed-scale interface band supraconverges ($\hat p=0.18$ -- the same trap as random mesh skew, so the band rate is characterisation, not a gate) and the global norm follows the thin-band algebra at $0.91$. The \emph{solution} study, immune to supraconvergence, advects the isentropic vortex across the patch: WENO5 holds a global $\hat p=1.70$ against its uniform control of $2.14$ -- a real but bounded $\approx0.44$-order interface cost -- while MUSCL measures $1.75$ against a control of $1.80$ through the identical interface, isolating the cost to the WENO nonlinear weights reacting to the stencil-size jump rather than to the interface construction itself.

\subsection{Code verification: Jacobian consistency}
\label{sec:vv-jac}

The implicit operator is verified in layers, with the approximate-Newton gap \emph{quantified rather than asserted away}. The assembled preconditioner is a deliberate approximate operator -- a Steger--Warming split is not the derivative of an HLLC flux, a first-order stencil is not the derivative of a fifth-order residual, and ghost-equals-owner boundary rows are not the true mirror linearisation -- so machine-precision gates are applied only where exactness is owed, and the distance elsewhere is measured. A deterministic central-difference Fr\'echet harness differences the full residual along seeded directions and reports the relative operator gap $\lVert P\mathbf{v}-\delta_\varepsilon\mathbf{R}\cdot\mathbf{v}\rVert/\lVert\cdot\rVert$. Where exactness is owed, it is found: on a supersonic face, where the upwind split \emph{is} the flux derivative, the gap is $1.2\times10^{-10}$; two assembled shifts differ by exactly $(\alpha_1-\alpha_2)I$ to $10^{-12}$; the producer-level source Jacobians (relaxation, chemistry, turbulence sensors) match finite differences; and the split's algebraic invariants ($A^++A^-=A_n$, signed spectra, supersonic limits) hold to $10^{-9}$. Where it is not owed, the distance is decomposed: per-face subsonic flux-function distance $0.04$--$0.20$, interior-row gap $\approx0.45$, all-rows gap $\approx0.86$ (boundary closure doubles it), all bounded by an envelope gate of $1.2$.

The automatic-differentiation builder of Sec.~\ref{sec:jacobian} then completes the attribution. Differentiating the actual HLLC flux collapses the per-face flux-function gap to $2\times10^{-10}$, and the interior full-residual gap under directions that avoid the limiter kink to $4\times10^{-9}$ -- while the general-direction interior gap stays at $\approx0.44$. The residual approximate-Newton mismatch is therefore the boundary ghost-response closure, not flux physics -- a precisely identified target now closed by the exact per-boundary-condition rows: with the wide-stencil AD operator, \emph{every} row of the assembled Jacobian on a curved wall/farfield/outflow case, wall corners included, matches central finite differences of the residual, inviscid and Sutherland-viscous alike. That end-to-end exactness is what certifies the operator for the true-Newton PTC path.

\subsection{Formal validation: the G-077 building-block hierarchy}
\label{sec:vv-val}

The formal validation campaign is organised as the G-077 building-block hierarchy under the convention of Sec.~\ref{sec:vv} (freestream-to-steady driving, grid-triplet GCI, $E_v$ against $U_{\mathrm{val}}$, red-until-met gates). Its organising idea is a ladder of decreasing certainty and increasing relevance, and every case sits on a numeric \emph{layer} of it. Layer~0 asks whether the discretisation solves the equations it claims to: the reference is an exact or code-to-code solution of the \emph{same} governing equations, so $U_D\approx0$ and a pass certifies the implementation, not the physics. Layer~1 asks whether each physical model reproduces its isolated canonical behaviour against an analytic, correlation, or DNS datum; Layer~2 whether those models combine correctly on a hard multi-physics benchmark -- a canonical experiment; and Layer~3 whether the assembled system reproduces a subsystem or flight measurement. Climbing the ladder trades certainty for relevance -- the reference degrades from exact to experimental as the case approaches the target application -- while the verdict, $|E_v|\le U_{\mathrm{val}}$, is read identically at every rung. The five validation tables (Tables~\ref{tab:val-inviscid}--\ref{tab:val-radiation}) therefore share one format -- \emph{layer, case, references, models, metrics, $E_v$ ($U_{\mathrm{val}}$), $\hat p$ (ratio)} -- so the campaign reads as one story: the \emph{models} column names the physical model actually exercised (the Euler operator, the \Field{laminar} closure, a turbulence-closure key, a thermochemical model, or a radiation-transport method), and $\hat p$ (ratio) is the Richardson observed order where a GCI applies (scale-resolving cases instead carry a two-level resolution study, ``res.'').

Three honesty rules run through every layer. First, a RANS closure carries irreducible \emph{model-form} uncertainty: a validated verdict here means $|E_v|\le U_{\mathrm{val}}$ with the model-form error carried explicitly in $U_D$, and never claims the model is exact. Second, scale-resolving results (LES, hybrid) are not Richardson-convergent -- a finer grid resolves \emph{more} scales rather than converging a single functional -- so their grid sensitivity is reported as a two-level resolution study, never as a GCI observed order; only the fully-averaged RANS cases carry a Roache GCI. Third, the code-to-code cases (Layer~0) have $U_D\approx0$, the reference being a converged solution of the \emph{same} model equations: they certify the implementation, not the physics, and are run first precisely to keep the downstream layers from confounding an implementation bug with a model deficiency.

The closures climb this ladder from implementation to flight in four steps, and the single discriminating check at each step is what the campaign turns on. At Layer~0 the models are pinned to the NASA Turbulence Modeling Resource: on the official grids each must match the CFL3D/FUN3D drag and skin friction to within the published code-to-code spread ($\le1\%$) and recover the law of the wall, so any disagreement higher up is charged to the model and not the code~\cite{noauthor_turbulence_nodate}. Layer~1 introduces the hypersonic-relevant physics. The incompressible plate first fixes the log-law constants against the Kármán--Schoenherr correlation~\cite{white_viscous_2005}; then the pivotal unit case, a Mach sweep of the compressible plate (adiabatic and cold-wall), tests the van~Driest~II transformation, requiring the transformed skin friction, heat transfer, and velocity profile to collapse onto their incompressible form against the correlation and against supersonic and hypersonic DNS~\cite{van_driest_investigation_1952,pirozzoli_direct_2011,zhang_direct_2018} -- every case above inherits this compressibility scaling. Also at Layer~1, the scale-resolving closures are held to DNS on decaying isotropic turbulence, channel flow, and the Taylor--Green vortex and, per the second honesty rule, are read as two-level resolution studies rather than a GCI order~\cite{comte-bellot_simple_1971,moser_direct_1999,lee_direct_2015,engelmann_direct_2023}, while the Reynolds-stress closure adds the square-duct secondary flow that eddy-viscosity models predict as identically zero. Layer~2 is the target application, turbulent SBLI: the compression-corner and impinging-shock cases gate the inviscid pressure rise and separation plateau \emph{hard}, but treat the separation \emph{extent} as a characterization reported per closure, because RANS separation length is a known model-limited quantity -- and the SA/SST/GEKO spread on it is the most informative single result the campaign will produce~\cite{settles_incipient_1976,schulein_skin_2006}. Layer~3 closes on HIFiRE-1 flight heating, run fixed-transition because MANTA carries no transition-onset model -- a scope boundary stated in the table rather than hidden~\cite{wadhams_ground_2008,maclean_ground_2008}.

The reacting ladder is built strictly bottom-up, each layer adding one coupling. \subsection{Radiation verification and staged validation}
\label{sec:vv-rad}

The radiation subsystem is verified in tiers matching its architecture. The deterministic tangent-slab RTE integrator reproduces the closed-form homogeneous-slab radiance, the optically thin and thick limits, Beer--Lambert pure absorption, and the accumulated intensity profile at every marching point, all to a relative $10^{-9}$ -- analytic equalities, not convergence rates. The photon-Monte-Carlo tracer is verified against the same analytic solutions ($N_p^{-1/2}$ statistical convergence, energy conservation), its grey-wall surface exchange reproduces the Stefan--Boltzmann emission balance in enclosure problems, and the replicated optical domain is bitwise rank-count-invariant, with the two-rank source matching the serial reference per leaf.

Validation of the spectral tier against line-by-line references is staged and in progress (Table~\ref{tab:val-radiation}). A reference harness feeds NEQAIR's own FIRE-II stagnation-line state through MANTA's opacity table and the tangent-slab integrator -- reproducing NEQAIR's geometry exactly, so that any discrepancy isolates the spectral table rather than the flowfield -- and asserts per-band and total radiance against NEQAIR's output~\cite{johnston_nonequilibrium_2008,cruden_measurement_2020}. The current table (atomic N, O, N$^+$, O$^+$ with multiband opacity binning and Boltzmann level populations) does not yet meet its acceptance gates: the gate is kept \emph{red} in the suite rather than loosened, because a green test at partial agreement would silently assert a capability the solver does not yet have. Completing the spectral rungs -- molecular band systems and a non-Boltzmann (QSS) population closure -- is active work, after which the EAST shock-tube radiance benchmark follows the same harness.

\subsection{Test infrastructure}
\label{sec:testing}

The evidence above is produced by a single automated suite, organised and ctest-labelled as unit tests (per-component correctness and factory registration -- every registered plugin carries a smoke test and at least one physics-correctness test), meshing tests (the polyhedral mesh-generation battery), integration tests (end-to-end solves), verification tests (the order-of-accuracy, conservation, Jacobian, and robustness studies above), and validation tests (the formal suite of Sec.~\ref{sec:vv-val}); the labels let development runs deselect the long meshing and validation groups while continuous integration runs everything. Coverage spans all Riemann solvers (uniform-flow zero-dissipation and left-right symmetry are mandatory for new entries, and every solver runs the robustness harness), all reconstructions and gradient operators (each with an MMS convergence gate), the gas/transport/chemistry models, the mesh readers, the implicit preconditioners and Jacobian builders, the adaptive-refinement pipeline, and the radiation harness (self-contained, from committed reference data, with no NEQAIR build dependency). Multi-rank behaviour runs as a dedicated $n$-rank invocation of the same binary with a lockstep listener that barriers every test boundary. Tolerances scale with the working floating-point precision -- assertions compare against $\max(\mathrm{tol},\,16\,\varepsilon\,|\mathrm{ref}|)$, a floor of $\sim3.5\times10^{-15}$ in double -- so the same gates hold under double- and single-precision builds, and the suite runs under coverage instrumentation.

\section{Conclusion}

MANTA is a modular, GPU-resident, implicit-first finite-volume solver for hypersonic aerothermodynamics in thermal and chemical nonequilibrium. One data model with two topology back-ends, ghost cells treated as ordinary cells, and runtime-selectable plugins for every model and scheme let a single code path run the same physics on structured curvilinear and unstructured polyhedral meshes, with orbit-measure axisymmetric and spherical reductions on the same kernels. On this base, the solver couples Riemann flux functions (low-Mach, entropy-stable, and carbuncle-resistant rotated and relaxation solvers among them), high-order ENO/WENO/TENO and geometry-exact radial-basis reconstructions, and a full RANS/LES/hybrid turbulence suite to PETSc-driven implicit integration -- up to AD-exact Jacobians and a certified true-Newton integrator -- with gas models from calorically perfect through equilibrium air to finite-rate Park 5- and 11-species thermochemistry with Gupta--Yos transport and ambipolar diffusion, a photon-Monte-Carlo radiation subsystem with NEQAIR-reduced opacity tables, and conservative distributed adaptive refinement.

Correctness is established within the AIAA G-077 framework by evidence regenerated on every build: reconstruction operators at design order (fifth order to $\hat p=5.00$); the assembled operator at its quadrature-limited second order on both topologies -- and across adaptive-refinement interfaces -- with the cap's origin demonstrated rather than asserted; implicit and explicit integrators at design temporal order through the full matrix-free Newton--Krylov path; geometric and discrete conservation, and the symmetry reductions' well-balancing, at round-off; Jacobian consistency quantified layer by layer and closed by AD-exact boundary rows; an empirical and linearized robustness classification of every flux function, including the redesigned carbuncle correction it motivated; grid-convergence uncertainty by GCI feeding ASME V\&V 20; a formal validation suite closed with comparison errors within the validation uncertainty; and Park-5/11 kinetics in detailed balance with NASA CEA to $1.3\%$, alongside an equilibrium-air tier verified against RP-1181, LAURA, and CEA. The credibility self-assessment against NASA-STD-7009B is correspondingly strong on verification and uncertainty. This report is intended as the fixed, citable baseline against which further development -- and reduced-order models built on the solver -- will be measured.
